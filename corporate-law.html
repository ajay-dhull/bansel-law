What is Corporate Law?

Corporate law governs the formation, operation, and management of businesses. It includes corporate governance, ensuring businesses meet legal requirements, protecting shareholder interests, managing risks, and assisting with commercial transactions. It also involves dispute resolution for businesses facing conflicts.
In Australia, the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) is the primary legislation regulating companies. Corporate lawyers ensure businesses meet their legal obligations under this Act, helping ensure long-term success.

Corporate Litigation and Dispute Resolution

Disputes can arise in any business, whether related to contracts, shareholders, or regulatory issues. Our corporate litigation services include:

  • Contract Disputes: Resolving breaches of contract and other commercial disagreements.
  • Director & Shareholder Disputes: Resolving conflicts between directors and shareholders, including issues of breach of duty and shareholder rights.
  • Fraud & Misrepresentation: Taking legal action for fraud, misrepresentation, and wrongful acts.
