Facing Assault Charges? Here's What You Need to Know

Being charged with assault is a serious criminal offence in Australia. A conviction can result in a criminal record, impacting your ability to secure employment, travel overseas, or even access certain opportunities.

If you have been charged with assault, it’s essential to get legal advice and representation as soon as possible. Our expert criminal lawyers are here to guide you. Contact us today for a free initial consultation.

Your Rights and the Prosecution's Role

In Australia, you are presumed innocent until proven guilty. It’s up to the prosecution to prove the charges beyond a reasonable doubt.

Types of Assault in Australia

  • Common Assault: Physical contact without consent that does not cause bodily harm.
  • Assault Occasioning Actual Bodily Harm (ABH): Results in injury to another person.
  • Assault Occasioning Grievous Bodily Harm (GBH): Involves serious or life-threatening injuries.

Penalties for Assault Offences in Australia

Penalties vary depending on the type of assault. Common penalties include imprisonment, fines, or community service. The court considers factors such as intent, harm caused, and whether there were aggravating factors.

Defences Against Assault Charges

Common defences include self-defence, consent, and intoxication. Our lawyers can help determine which defences may apply to your case.

Special Categories of Assault

Assaulting a police officer or committing assault in a domestic setting can lead to harsher penalties.
