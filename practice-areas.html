Eligibility Criteria for Australian Visas

  • Student Visas require you to be enrolled in an approved educational institution.
  • Work Visas often require an employment offer or sponsorship.
  • Family Visas may require a sponsor who is an Australian citizen or permanent resident.

Types of Australian Visas

Here are some common visa categories available to individuals seeking to enter or remain in Australia:

  • Visitor and Tourism Visas
  • Study and Training Visas
  • Work Visas
  • Family and Partner Visas
  • Permanent Residency Visas
  • Humanitarian and Refugee Visas
  • Bridging Visas

Legislation Governing Immigration in Australia

The Migration Act 1958 (Cth) is the primary legislation regulating Australia’s migration system. It outlines visa requirements, eligibility criteria, obligations of visa applicants and sponsors, procedures for handling appeals and reviews, and professional standards for migration agents and lawyers.

The Role of a Registered Migration Agent

When working with a migration agent, ensure they are registered with the Office of the Migration Agents Registration Authority (OMARA). Registered agents are required to stay updated on current laws and practices and adhere to a strict Code of Conduct, ensuring ethical and professional standards.

What is Property Law?

Property law governs the ownership, use, and transfer of real estate and personal property. It includes legal matters related to residential, commercial, and industrial properties, as well as property disputes, leasing, and development. Property law is subject to state and territory-specific regulations, making it essential to have knowledgeable legal experts who understand the local laws applicable to your case.

Our Expertise in Property Law

We offer comprehensive legal services in property law, including:

  • Residential and Commercial Transactions: Buying or selling residential, commercial, or industrial properties. Drafting and reviewing sale contracts, including special conditions. Managing conveyancing for smooth property transfers.
  • Property Leasing: Drafting and reviewing commercial and retail lease agreements. Negotiating lease terms for landlords and tenants. Resolving disputes related to leasing arrangements.
  • Property Development and Subdivisions: Legal advice on property development, including zoning and planning regulations. Assisting with subdivision applications and approvals. Preparing development agreements.
  • Strata and Community Titles: Legal assistance with strata title disputes. Advising on strata by-laws and management agreements. Resolving disputes between owners and strata corporations.
  • Property Disputes: Boundary disputes and adverse possession claims. Co-ownership and joint ownership disputes. Property damage claims and recovery of unpaid rents.
  • Mortgages and Financing: Drafting and reviewing mortgage agreements. Advising on property refinancing and security arrangements.
  • Compulsory Acquisition and Compensation: Representing clients affected by government acquisition of property. Negotiating fair compensation under state-specific acquisition laws.
  • Foreign Investment in Property: Advising foreign investors on FIRB compliance for property purchases. Assisting with applications to the Foreign Investment Review Board (FIRB).
